Searching for a Connection between Migration and Urbanization

The Meditteranean

Europe has led a wave of right wing populism and the implementation of stringent anti-immigration policies at the expense of the well-being of migrants and asylum seekers. Far right groups claim that an overwhelming "invasive" number of migrants come to Europe through the Mediterranean.

In mid-year 2020, the International Organization for Migration documented that out of 280 598 105 migrants worldwide, 40 558 200 went to the Mediterranean.

A closer look at this topic is important to ensure that cities and metropolitan areas have the necessary infrastructure and services to welcome new residents.

Italy has received particular attention as it is spoken about as a 'point of entry' to the continent. The far right has capilized on citizens' frustrations regarding high unemployment rates and corruption to periodically close the border to asylum seekers.
